Fellowship Background

What is a Marie Skłodowska-Curie ITN Fellowship?

European Commission · Horizon 2020
Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions
Innovative Training Network (ITN) — EU Programme for Research Excellence

The Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) represent the European Commission's flagship funding programme for researcher mobility and training. Named after the two-time Nobel laureate Marie Curie, the programme is widely regarded as one of the most prestigious research fellowships in the world.

Within the MSCA framework, the Innovative Training Network (ITN) is the most competitive and selective category. An ITN brings together universities, national laboratories, and industry partners across multiple countries to form a consortium that trains a small cohort of Early Stage Researchers (ESRs) — typically fewer than 15 per network, selected globally.

ITN fellows are not simply PhD students — they are expected to conduct original research across multiple partner institutions, publish in peer-reviewed journals, and contribute to science at the European frontier. The acceptance rate is typically below 1–2% of applicants, making an ITN fellowship comparable in selectivity to Rhodes or Gates Cambridge scholarships.

The Company

Chronic care is a control problem. Not a treatment problem.

Orcasho is a closed-loop biochemical control system that corrects chronic gut dysfunction by stabilizing digestive chemistry at the ionic level — not suppressing symptoms with acid blockers.

Every patient is encoded as a 15-dimensional ionic state vector (H⁺, HCO₃⁻, Na⁺, K⁺, Cl⁻, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, NH₄⁺, CO₂, ROS, pH, O₂⁻, Zn²⁺, Fe²⁺, Mn²⁺). Interventions are selected by cosine similarity between each compound's ionic signature and the patient's required directional movement. Every 20 days, the system reassesses and recalibrates.

"Supplements are not the control variable. Ions are. Supplements are delivery vehicles for ionic influence."

— Orcasho Core Architecture Principle
